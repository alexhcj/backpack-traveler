<p class="md-paragraph">
  <span class="md-first-letter">D</span>ublin combines old-world charm and modern buzz. From Guinness and Georgian architecture to art and live music, here are fifteen unforgettable experiences that define the Irish capital.
</p>

<div class="md-space-lg"></div>
<h4 class="md-list-title">The 15 Best Things to Do in Dublin</h4>
<div class="md-space-lg"></div>
<ul class="md-list col-1">
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>1. Visit Trinity College & The Book of Kells</b> – Step inside Ireland’s oldest university and admire the world-famous illuminated manuscript in the Long Room Library.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>2. Explore Dublin Castle</b> – Once the heart of British rule in Ireland, now an art and cultural hub filled with history.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>3. Enjoy a Pint at the Guinness Storehouse</b> – Learn how Ireland’s most iconic beer is brewed, then sip it at the Gravity Bar overlooking the city.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>4. Wander through Temple Bar</b> – Dublin’s cultural quarter packed with live music, cobblestone alleys, and vibrant pubs.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>5. Visit Kilmainham Gaol Museum</b> – A moving look into Ireland’s path to independence inside this former prison.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>6. Relax in Phoenix Park</b> – One of Europe’s largest city parks, home to deer and Dublin Zoo.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>7. Listen to Live Irish Folk Music</b> – Pubs like O’Donoghue’s or The Cobblestone keep traditional tunes alive nightly.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>8. Discover St. Patrick’s Cathedral</b> – A masterpiece of Gothic architecture and Ireland’s national cathedral.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>9. Tour the Jameson Distillery</b> – Taste the smoothness of Ireland’s finest whiskey and learn its centuries-old craft.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>10. Explore the National Museum of Ireland</b> – Dive into Ireland’s archaeology, art, and folklore — all under one roof.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>11. Take a Literary Pub Crawl</b> – Follow the footsteps of Joyce, Yeats, and Wilde through Dublin’s storytelling taverns.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>12. Walk the Howth Cliff Path</b> – Stunning sea views, fresh air, and maybe a seal or two near the harbor.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>13. Visit the GPO Museum</b> – Learn about the 1916 Easter Rising in the city’s historic General Post Office.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>14. Stroll Merrion Square Park</b> – Admire colorful Georgian doors and Oscar Wilde’s statue beneath leafy trees.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>15. Enjoy Street Performers on Grafton Street</b> – Dublin’s heartbeat of shopping, music, and laughter.</li>
</ul>
