<p class="md-paragraph">
  <span class="md-first-letter">F</span>rom the beaches to the stadiums, Australians bring their unique energy to every game played around the world. Sport is deeply woven into the nation’s identity — a mix of passion, teamwork, and that laid-back Aussie humor.
</p>

<p class="md-excerpt">Here are the world’s 10 most popular sports — through the eyes of an Aussie.</p>
<h4 class="md-list-title">Top 10 Global Sports Loved Down Under</h4>
<div class="md-space-lg"></div>
<ul class="md-list col-1">
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>1. Football (Soccer)</b> – The global game continues to rise in Australia, with A-League fans and young players following European clubs passionately.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>2. Cricket</b> – A national obsession. The Ashes series between Australia and England remains one of the oldest and fiercest rivalries in sports.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>3. Rugby</b> – Both Rugby League and Rugby Union dominate weekends, especially in New South Wales and Queensland.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>4. Tennis</b> – The Australian Open in Melbourne draws the world’s best, turning January into a festival of aces and backhands.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>5. Surfing</b> – With over 35,000 km of coastline, surfing isn’t just a hobby — it’s a lifestyle rooted in Australia’s coastal culture.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>6. Basketball</b> – The NBL is growing rapidly, and Aussie stars like Patty Mills and Josh Giddey have carried that momentum into the NBA.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>7. Golf</b> – From the dunes of Tasmania to the coastal greens of Queensland, Australia breeds world-class golfers and scenic courses.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>8. Athletics</b> – Olympic pride runs deep; from Cathy Freeman’s gold to new generations chasing records.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>9. Aussie Rules Football</b> – Uniquely Australian, AFL combines speed, skill, and fearless tackles. The MCG Grand Final is a national event.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b>10. Swimming</b> – “The Dolphins” dominate world competitions — from Ian Thorpe to Emma McKeon, swimming defines Australia’s Olympic legacy.</li>
</ul>
