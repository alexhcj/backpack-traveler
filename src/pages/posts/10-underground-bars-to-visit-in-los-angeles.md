<div class="md-space"></div>

<p class="md-paragraph"><span class="md-first-letter">L</span>os Angeles may be known for its sunny rooftop lounges, but some of the city’s best bars are hidden below the surface. Tucked behind unmarked doors and neon-lit basements, these underground gems offer craft cocktails, moody lighting, and that irresistible speakeasy allure.</p>

<div class="md-space-xl"></div>
<h4 class="md-list-title">Top 10 Underground Bars in Los Angeles</h4>

<div class="md-space-lg"></div>
<ul class="md-list col-1">
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">1. The Varnish</b> – A Downtown L.A. staple hidden behind Cole’s French Dip. Expect jazz, dim lights, and perfectly balanced classics.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">2. Lock & Key</b> – In Koreatown, you’ll enter through a wall of fake doors before finding this intimate cocktail den.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">3. Good Times at Davey Wayne’s</b> – A ‘70s house party vibe accessed through a vintage refrigerator door. Think disco, denim, and dancing.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">4. The Wolves</b> – A restored 1910s bar with apothecary vibes and seasonal cocktails in Downtown L.A.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">5. Employees Only</b> – A New York import with a hidden tarot room and impeccable craft drinks in West Hollywood.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">6. Bar Jackalope</b> – A whiskey lover’s dream hidden behind Seven Grand; reservations and respect for the quiet atmosphere are key.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">7. The Basement Tavern</b> – Beneath the Victorian in Santa Monica, offering live music and nostalgic charm.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">8. The Spear Steak & Seafood House Lounge</b> – Downtown’s corporate front hides a classy basement bar for serious cocktail aficionados.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">9. No Vacancy</b> – A vintage-themed speakeasy entered through a secret staircase in a Victorian mansion near Hollywood Boulevard.</li>
  <li class="md-list-item"><b class="md-bold">10. The Edison</b> – Set in an old power plant, this legendary L.A. spot mixes industrial aesthetics, cabaret acts, and cocktails worth the hype.</li>
</ul>

<blockquote class="md-blockquote">
  “L.A.’s true nightlife magic isn’t on the Strip — it’s underground, where stories are whispered over candlelight and bourbon.”
</blockquote>

<p class="md-paragraph">Most of these bars keep their entrances discreet, so checking their Instagram or websites for reservations is essential. Dress codes tend toward smart-casual, and many spots offer live jazz or themed nights. Whether you’re chasing prohibition vibes or just want a quiet corner away from the Hollywood glare, these venues redefine what nightlife in Los Angeles feels like.</p>

<div class="md-space-xl"></div>
<h4 class="md-list-title">Suggested Bar Crawl Route</h4>

<div class="md-space-lg"></div>
<ul class="md-list col-1">
  <li class="md-list-item">Start in <b>Downtown L.A.</b> (The Varnish, The Wolves, The Edison)</li>
  <li class="md-list-item">Head to <b>Koreatown</b> (Lock & Key)</li>
  <li class="md-list-item">Finish in <b>Hollywood & West Hollywood</b> (No Vacancy, Employees Only)</li>
</ul>

<p class="md-paragraph"> Los Angeles has no shortage of flashy nightlife, but its underground bars offer something more intimate — a blend of history, mystery, and craftsmanship. Whether you’re sipping whiskey under vintage chandeliers or discovering a bar through a hidden door, these tucked-away gems remind us that the city’s true spirit thrives off the beaten path. Next time you’re in L.A., skip the mainstream and head underground — that’s where the real stories begin. </p>
